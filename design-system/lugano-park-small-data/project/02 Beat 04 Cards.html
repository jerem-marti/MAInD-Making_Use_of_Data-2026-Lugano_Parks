Beat 04 · Six lenses Act I · scrolly-telling
Lens 01

Experiential–Emotional

how a place made someone feel

This is the most personal lens. It captures the inner state of the visitor — the emotional residue left by a park visit. Not what was there, but what it did to you.

When someone calls a park magical or peaceful, they aren't describing a feature. They're describing a transformation. This lens listens for exactly that.

beautiful·peaceful·wonderful·relaxing·magical
bello·tranquillo·meraviglioso·rilassante·magico
Lens 02

Sensory–Environmental

what it looked, sounded, smelled like

Before you have time to form a feeling, your senses are already at work. This lens captures the raw perceptual character of a park — its palette, its soundscape, its textures.

It's the difference between arriving somewhere and landing somewhere. The air, the light, the quiet that tells you immediately what kind of place this is.

green·quiet·clean·shaded·view
verde·silenzioso·pulito·ombreggiato·vista
Lens 03

Action

what people did there

Some parks are experienced passively. Others are experienced in motion. This lens captures the kinetic dimension — the activities and practices that make a park what it is.

A high Action weight is the signature of a park that's used, not just visited. People don't pass through; they walk, picnic, photograph, play.

walk·relax·picnic·visit·play
passeggiare·rilassarsi·picnic·visitare·giocare
Lens 04

Relational Context

who was there, who it's for

A park is not just a place — it's a social context. This lens listens for the people in the picture: who's writing the review, who they brought, who they noticed around them.

It tells you whether a park belongs to families with strollers, to elderly locals on a regular bench, or to tourists passing through. That social identity shapes the experience as much as the landscape does.

children·families·couples·dogs·groups
bambini·famiglie·coppie·cani·gruppi
Lens 05

Infrastructure–Amenities

what was physically there

This is the most concrete of the six. It names the things that are actually there — benches, fountains, playgrounds, paths. The physical fabric of the park.

A high Infrastructure weight often signals a park well-equipped and frequently used for specific purposes — or a park whose physical distinctiveness makes it memorable enough to mention.

bench·playground·trees·path·fountain
panchina·parco giochi·alberi·sentiero·fontana
Lens 06

Tension–Complaint

what disappointed

No park is perfect, and honest portraits require acknowledging the friction. This lens captures the negative signals — the crowding, the neglect, the small failures that chip away at the experience.

A park with very low Tension is genuinely exceptional. Most have some, and that matters. Suppressing it would make the aura a flattery, not a portrait.

dirty·crowded·neglected·broken·noisy
sporco·affollato·trascurato·rotto·rumoroso
